# HIPAA scope and No-PHI policy

The standard PneuNexus Ops deployment is an infrastructure documentation and maintenance system, not a patient-record system. It must not collect patient names, MRNs, birth dates, diagnoses, specimen identifiers, prescriptions, patient photographs, or other PHI. Users must keep descriptions, notes, attachments, imports, incidents, and maintenance records infrastructure-only.

Configurable pattern rules may warn or block text resembling prohibited data. They are a supplemental safeguard, not a guarantee. Governance, training, monitoring, vendor agreements, access controls, and incident handling remain necessary.

PneuNexus Ops is designed to support healthcare security and compliance programs when deployed, configured, operated and contracted appropriately. It is not “HIPAA certified,” and software features alone do not make an organization compliant. If a future use case changes the data scope, stop and perform legal, privacy, security, vendor, and architecture review before collection.
